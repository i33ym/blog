The answer to the question of "What is the meaning of life ?" is almost in the question itself. It is to live. That might sound over-simplified or even absurdly vague. Well... Firstly, I intend no entertainment with words today. Secondly, humans are complex beings and they like to complicate the stuff. Since we do not no longer use words in their original meaning and instead we try to invent new words, let me explain what I mean by "live" without inventing a new word for it.

Imagine, your teacher asks you "Have you read Rumi's poetry ?". Although, in reality, you might have just scanned through his poetry, we all know that you've not actually read the poetry. Because the original and true meaning of reading is to read with understanding. I know how to read Arabic. But if you give me the Qur'an, I cannot read it. I mean I can "read" it. But it is not really called reading. Because although I can utter or "read" the words written in Qur'an, I cannot understand the points it is making. Sometimes I hear people using the phrase "true repentence". There's no need to invent new words to explain the old concepts. Repentence itself means true repentence.

In the same sense, living means living __right__. It means living to have the best human experience possible. But how do you know something is __right__ ? Well... You know the __right__ from the __wrong__. :)
